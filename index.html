Junjie Zhang (张俊杰)

junjie.zhang@sri.utoronto.ca

I am currently a post-doctoral research fellow at Sunnybrook Health Research Centre and University of Toronto, where I carry out research on radiomics/quanitative imaging and lead the effort on building machine intellegent systems for computer-aided diagnosis of prostate cancer. Previously, I was a project research scientist at Industrial SkyWorks Inc., building computer vision and machine learnign systems for automatic anomaly detection from UAV-based thermal imaging.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Geomatics Engineering at York University, advised by Prof. Gunho Sohn. During my graduate study I've worked/interned in both academic and industrial funded projects. I obtained a M.Sc. degree in Geoinformatics from University of Twente, the Netherlands. I also hold a bachelar and a master degree from Wuhan University, China.

I host several open soucrce projects on Github as hobby. I authored SLICO#, a SLICO superpixel segmentation algorithm in C#, and MatIO.NET, a .Net library for reading and writing Matlab MAT-Files.

I do have a LinkedIn profile.

Research

My research involves applying machine learning in a number of quantitative fields, including medical imaging, health informatics, computer vision, and geomatics engineering. I also actively participate in deep learning and data analytics research in biomedical engineering related clinical projects.

My current research topics include:

  • Learning better structures for image feature extraction.
  • Explaining human generalization behavior with visually grounded cogscience models.
  • Making large-scale vision feasible and affordable.
